HUMAN PSYCHOLOGY RESEARCH — WINNING vs LOSING MINDSET
Real citations for iCard generation

1. LEARNED HELPLESSNESS (Seligman & Maier, 1967)
- Original experiment: dogs exposed to inescapable shock stopped trying to escape even when escape became possible
- Human application (Hiroto & Seligman, 1975): humans exposed to uncontrollable noise failed to solve subsequent anagram tasks
- Key finding: "The learned helplessness effect is a failure to escape — a more profound debilitation than failure to prevent"
- Reformulated model (Abramson, Seligman & Teasdale, 1978): attributional style determines helplessness — internal, stable, global attributions create depression
- Students who repeatedly fail conclude they are incapable of improving (Wikipedia/educational research)
- Source: Journal of Abnormal Psychology, 87(1), 49-74

2. DEMING'S PSYCHOLOGY OF QUALITY (W. Edwards Deming, 1993)
- System of Profound Knowledge includes Psychology as 4th pillar
- "One is born with intrinsic motivation, self-esteem, dignity, cooperation, curiosity, joy in learning"
- External systems (ranking, annual reviews, blame) DESTROY intrinsic motivation
- Win-lose systems create fear. Fear kills quality.
- "Drive out fear, so that everyone may work effectively" (Point 8 of 14)
- Quality is free (Crosby, 1979) — it is the LACK of quality that costs
- Source: The New Economics, MIT Press, 1993

3. CORTISOL & DOPAMINE — THE NEUROCHEMISTRY
- Repeated failure → elevated cortisol (stress hormone) → impaired prefrontal cortex function
- McEwen (2010): "allostatic load" — failure to habituate to repeated stressors leads to persistent elevation of stress mediators
- Source: Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences, cited 2,509 times
- Dopamine: success releases dopamine → reinforces successful behaviours → builds confidence
- Failure depletes dopamine → reduces motivation → creates avoidance behaviour
- Nature (2017): fatigue modulates dopamine availability, promotes flexible choice reversals
- Decision fatigue (Baumeister, 2011): repeated decisions deplete willpower, leading to poorer choices

4. WINNING PSYCHOLOGY
- Winning reinforces successful behaviours, builds confidence, enhances motivation
- "Winner effect" (Robertson, 2012): testosterone rises after wins, cortisol drops → creates positive feedback loop
- Flow state (Csikszentmihalyi, 1990): optimal performance occurs when challenge matches skill — repeated failure pushes person OUT of flow into anxiety
- Toyota Production System: "Stop the line" — fix defect immediately, never pass downstream. Jidoka principle.
- Right first time = compound confidence. Rework = compound erosion.
