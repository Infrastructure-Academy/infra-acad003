## What the Chart Shows

Earth Overshoot Day marks the date each year when humanity has used more from nature than the planet can renew in the entire year. The chart tracks this date from 1971 to 2025, revealing a relentless acceleration of ecological deficit.

| Decade | Approximate Overshoot Day | Trend |
|--------|--------------------------|-------|
| 1971 | Late December | Near equilibrium |
| 1975 | Late November | First measurable overshoot |
| 1980 | Early November | Accelerating |
| 1985 | Late October | Steady advance |
| 1990 | Mid October | Continued |
| 1995 | Early October | Continued |
| 2000 | Late September | Sharp acceleration |
| 2005 | Late August | Continued |
| 2010 | Early August | Continued |
| 2015 | Mid August | Slight plateau |
| 2020 | August 22 (COVID rebound) | Temporary reversal |
| 2025 | **July 24** | Resumed advance |

The red portion of each bar represents the ecological deficit — the months of the year during which humanity is consuming beyond Earth's regenerative capacity. As of 2025, we require the equivalent of **1.8 Earths** to sustain current consumption patterns.

## About Global Footprint Network

**Global Footprint Network** is an international research organization founded in 2003 by Mathis Wackernagel. Headquartered in Oakland, California, it operates as a non-profit (NGO) dedicated to advancing the science of sustainability through the **Ecological Footprint** — a resource accounting framework that measures how much nature we use versus how much we have.

**Core Mission:** To help end ecological overshoot by making ecological limits central to decision-making at every level — from individuals to nations.

**Key Contributions:**
- Created and maintains the **National Footprint and Biocapacity Accounts** for over 200 countries
- Partnered with the **Footprint Data Foundation** and **York University** for peer-reviewed data
- Launched **Earth Overshoot Day** as a global awareness campaign
- Provides data to the United Nations, World Bank, and national governments
- Website: https://www.footprintnetwork.org/
