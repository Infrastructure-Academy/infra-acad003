## 2. The Original Formulation

The 4 Perennial Threats were first codified in the ICUT framework (Doc 4 / game data) as the horizontal weft threads of the Civilisational Loom — the forces that test every relay. They existed in two layers:

| Layer | C1 | C2 | C3 | C4 |
|-------|----|----|----|----|
| **Academic** | Conflagration | Consumption | Conflict | Climate |
| **Simple** | War | Famine | Disease | Existential |

These were defined as the content of **ICUT Framing Pillar 4: Threats**, answering the question *"What threatens sustainability?"* The glossary entry read:

> "The 4 Perennial Threats that run horizontally through the Loom. These are the forces that challenge every civilisation: War, Disease, Famine, Existential."

The framework page on the Infrastructure Academy website codified them as:

> "Conflagration (War), Contagion (Disease), Consumption (Famine), Climate (Existential)"

Note: even in this "original" formulation, **Contagion** had already appeared as the academic term for Disease — the seed was already in the emulsion.

## 4. What Changed and Why It Matters

The evolution from the original to the emergent formulation reveals three significant refinements:

**4.1 Conflagration → Conflict**

The original "Conflagration" carried connotations of fire and burning — a subset of destruction. "Conflict" is broader, encompassing war, invasion, ideological warfare, cyber-warfare, and the destruction of records (cultural erasure). The shift from a physical metaphor to a systemic one reflects the framework's maturation.

**4.2 Consumption → Cost**

"Consumption" (Famine) described the symptom — resources consumed. "Cost" describes the mechanism — economic failure, resource depletion, the denominator that weakens all signals. Cost is the universal language of resistance. Every infrastructure decision is ultimately a cost decision. This is the sharpest upgrade: it connects the 4Cs directly to the engineering and economic reality of infrastructure.

**4.3 Crisis → Contagion (confirmed)**

"Contagion" was already present in the framework page as the academic term for Disease, but it was not the primary label. In the emergent formulation, Contagion takes primacy — and its scope expands beyond biological plague to include ideological contagion, misinformation cascades, and societal collapse. In the age of social media, contagion is no longer just a virus — it is any self-replicating destructive pattern.

**4.4 Climate (retained)**

Climate remains unchanged — the slow horseman that reshapes everything over decades and centuries. Its stability across both formulations confirms it was already at the right level of abstraction.

The D100_PRINCIPIA_EXTRACTION formalised the relationship between the 4Cs and signal strength:

> **S = (A × P) / β**

Where:

| Variable | Meaning | D100 Definition |

| **A** | Amplitude = REACH | How far the signal travels. The breadth of influence. The radius of impact. |
| **P** | Persistence = DURATION | How long the signal endures. The lifespan of knowledge. Measured in centuries. |
| **β** | Resistance = THE 4Cs | Conflict · Climate · Contagion · Cost. The forces that destroy signal. |

The imperative that emerges from this equation is stated explicitly:

> *"Maximise reach. Maximise duration. Minimise resistance. This is the infrastructure imperative."*

This is the entire thesis of the Infrastructure Academy compressed into a single sentence. The 4Cs are not just threats — they are the denominator of civilisation itself. Minimise the denominator. That is the entire game.
