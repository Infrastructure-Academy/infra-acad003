Interpretation — Why This Song, This Moment

Fish wrote Fugazi as a condemnation of societal indifference — a world sleepwalking through decay, “drowning in the real,” distracted by “aural contraceptives aborting pregnant conversation.” The song moves through London’s broken infrastructure — Piccadilly line rat race, Brixton chess, Embankment’s homeless — cataloguing a civilisation that has stopped building and started consuming itself.

The climax is not despair. It is a demand: “Where are the prophets, where are the visionaries, where are the poets?” Fish is not asking rhetorically. He is calling them into existence. And then he dedicates the song to them — to the poets, the prophets, the visionaries. He is saying: you are the answer to the question the song asks.
