## 1. PURPOSE AND INTENT

This document records the complete process, lessons learned, and strategic intent behind the iAAi IP & Equations Register Card Deck. It exists so that future deck editions, card series, and trading card products can be produced without re-learning the process from scratch. Every upgrade, new series, or format adaptation should begin by reading this playbook.

The card deck is not merely a novelty product. It is a **teaching tool**, a **marketing asset**, and a **revenue stream** that encodes the entire iAAi intellectual property portfolio into a portable, collectible, and tradeable format. Each card must be **self-explanatory for a first-time learner** — no prior knowledge assumed, no jargon unexplained.

---

## 2. THE GOLDEN RULE

> **Every card must stand alone.** A student picking up a single card for the first time must understand what it says without needing to read any other card, any website, or any book. If the author (Nigel) doesn't understand it at first glance, neither will the buyer. Cards that require explanation are cards that will generate complaints, not sales.

## 3. QUALITY ASSURANCE PROCESS

The following process must be followed for every deck edition:

### Step 1: Source Audit
Before creating or updating any card, conduct a **full cross-reference audit** across ALL authoritative sources:

| Source | What to Extract | Priority |
|--------|----------------|----------|
| RECALL Master Ledger (all blocks) | Exact equations, definitions, context, origin stories | PRIMARY |
| Equations Register webpage | Current published equations (EQ-001 to EQ-038+) | PRIMARY |
| Main website (site.html) | Frameworks, acronyms, IP references | SECONDARY |
| iCard Gallery / Finance-Admin pages | Current card content, pricing, download links | SECONDARY |
| Deck Design Document (ip_equations_deck_design.md) | Card mappings, suit assignments, tier structure | REFERENCE |
| Nigel's direct corrections and feedback | Overrides everything else | ABSOLUTE |

### Step 2: Cross-Reference Matrix
Build a complete matrix: **every equation/IP** mapped against **every source** where it appears. Flag:
- Inconsistencies between sources (different expansions, different formulas)
- Missing items (in RECALL but not on website, or vice versa)
- New discoveries (equations found in RECALL blocks not yet catalogued)

### Step 3: Correction Manifest
Compile a card-by-card manifest showing:
- Current card content (what it says now)
- Correct card content (what it should say, with full self-explanatory text)
- Error cause (how the mistake was made)
- Source reference (which RECALL block or document provides the correct version)

### Step 4: Approval Gate
**Present the correction manifest to Nigel for approval BEFORE regenerating any cards.** Do not assume corrections are correct — the author's intent overrides all other sources.

### Step 5: Batch Regeneration
Regenerate all corrected cards in a single batch. Maintain visual consistency (same art style, same border, same colour scheme). Every card in the deck must look like it belongs to the same family.

### Step 6: PDF Assembly and Website Update
Rebuild the complete PDF. Update all website references (images, download links, card counts). Upload to CDN. Update the equations register page if any equations were corrected.

### Step 7: BETA Label
Until the deck has been fully verified by the author and at least one external reviewer, all cards and the PDF must carry a **BETA** label. This protects against liability and creates a natural upgrade path to "First Edition" once fully verified.

## 4. CARD DESIGN SPECIFICATIONS

### Visual Style
- **Border:** Art-deco gold, matching Tecton Trump Cards D52 series
- **Background:** Navy/dark blue (#0a1628)
- **Text:** Gold (#ffd700) for titles, equations, and borders
- **White text** for descriptions and context
- **Card size:** Standard playing card ratio (2.5" × 3.5" / 63.5mm × 88.9mm)
- **Resolution:** Minimum 300 DPI for print production

### Card Layout (Top to Bottom)
1. **Card value and suit symbol** (top corners)
2. **Equation ID** (e.g., EQ-001, IP-03)
3. **Suit symbol** (centred)
4. **Equation/Formula** (large, prominent, gold text)
5. **Decorative divider**
6. **Card name** (bold, descriptive)
7. **Self-explanatory description** (2-4 lines, plain English, no jargon)
8. **IP cross-reference** (bottom, smaller text)
9. **Card value and suit symbol** (bottom corners, inverted)
10. **Footer:** "IP & EQUATIONS REGISTER" + "BETA" label

### Content Rules
- **Acronyms:** Always expand fully on the card. Never assume the reader knows what FITS, ICUT, IUMC, etc. stand for.
- **Equations:** Show the full equation AND explain what each variable means.
- **Numbers:** Show the calculation, not just the result. "12,000 / 4,500,000,000 = 0.00000267" is better than "β = 2.667 × 10⁻⁶".
- **Context:** One sentence explaining WHY this equation matters to infrastructure/civilisation.
- **Units:** Always describe units for physics equations (E = h × f: Energy in Joules, h = Planck's constant in J·s, f = frequency in Hz).

The IP & Equations Register deck is the **first** in a planned series of card products. The format is deliberately modular — the same visual style, card dimensions, and production process can be applied to multiple series.

### 6.2 Top Trumps Model (Tecton Trump Cards)

Research reference: **Top Trumps** (Winning Moves UK) — competitive card game where players compare numerical attributes. Each card has 5-6 measurable stats. Player picks a stat they think will beat the opponent's card.

**iAAi Tecton Trump adaptation:**
- Each card = one infrastructure project or system
- Stats could include: Year Built, Cost (adjusted), Length/Scale, Workers, Impact Score, Survival Contribution
- Categories: Ancient Wonders, Roman Infrastructure, Silk Road, Industrial Revolution, Modern Megaprojects, Future Concepts
- Cross-references to the 12 Relays and 4 Modes

### 6.3 Trading Cards Model (Sports Card Analogy)

Research reference: **Sports trading cards** (Topps, Panini, Upper Deck) — collectible cards with player stats, rarity tiers, and secondary market value.

**iAAi Trading Card adaptation:**
- **Scholar Cards:** The 17 ICE Matrix figures as "players" with IQ/EQ/CQ stats, Volume score, Dice Position, and historical achievements
- **Relay Cards:** Key inventions and discoveries per relay, with date, impact rating, and relay contribution score
- **BitPoint Cards:** Earned through platform engagement (ICUT model completion), tradeable between players
- **Rarity tiers:** Common (earned through basic play), Rare (earned through mastery), Legendary (earned through contribution/translation/validation)
- **Secondary market:** Players can trade cards within the platform, creating a micro-economy that reinforces engagement

### 6.4 BitPoint Integration

BitPoints (the platform's reward currency) connect directly to the card ecosystem:
- **Earning:** Complete ICUT stages → earn BitPoints → unlock card packs
- **Trading:** Trade cards with other players using BitPoints as currency
- **Collecting:** Complete sets for bonus rewards (e.g., complete all 12 Relay cards = Master Weaver achievement)
- **Staking:** Hold rare cards for passive BitPoint generation (incentivises long-term engagement)

This creates a **closed-loop economy** where learning → earning → collecting → trading → more learning.

## 9. ERRORS LOG — LESSONS LEARNED

### Edition: BETA v1 (March 2026)

| Error | Cause | Fix | Prevention |
|-------|-------|-----|------------|
| FITS expanded as "Framework for Infrastructure Thinking & Strategy" instead of "Feeler, Intuit, Thinker, Strategist" | Deck design doc had wrong expansion; not cross-referenced with RECALL | Regenerate card with correct expansion | Always verify acronym expansions against RECALL, not just design doc |
| ICE Matrix missing Haptic Quotient (HQ) and Ventral Origin | New concepts from Block 354 not captured in original design | Add separate cards or expand existing ICE card | Audit ALL RECALL blocks, not just the ones referenced in design doc |
| BRI and 4ECL listed as "our equations" when they are external references | Design doc included them without distinguishing ownership | Replace with actual iAAi equations | Always verify: "Is this OUR equation or someone else's?" |
| Scientific notation used instead of explicit fractions | Convenience over clarity | Show the division: 12,000/4,500,000,000 | Always show the human-readable calculation |
| Cards condensed/combined to save space | Agent prioritised efficiency over learner experience | Every concept gets its own card | Golden Rule: every card must stand alone |
| Pythagorean card only showed basic triple | Incomplete extraction from Block 345 | Show full equation set (squares, cubes, products) | Extract COMPLETE content from each RECALL block |
| Missing Ventral Origin, Death Spiral, Entropy, Resilience Quotient | Not in original 38-equation list | Add as new cards or replacements | Cross-reference RECALL blocks for concepts beyond the original 38 |
